Refine the customer-facing wording throughout the Application Insights page. This is a copywriting and UX polish only. Do not modify any backend logic, prompts, JSON schema, API routes, or analysis behavior.

1. Replace formal language with simpler English

Throughout the report:

Replace "Your application indicates..." with "Your application shows..."
Replace "A consular officer may explore..." with "During your interview, you may be asked about this because..." or "A consular officer may ask about this because..."
Remove unnecessary formal or legal wording wherever possible.
2. Remove unnecessary legal terminology

Instead of:

previous B1/B2 visa refusal under section 214(b)

Use:

previous visa refusal

The legal reference does not help applicants understand their application.

3. Make explanations warmer and easier to understand

Rewrite the "Why this may come up" sections using simple, conversational English.

Examples:

Instead of:

"...to understand your professional stability..."

Use:

"...to better understand your work experience and how it relates to your trip."

Instead of:

"...whether your financial situation supports your travel plans."

Use:

"...to better understand how you plan to pay for your trip."

Instead of:

"...to ensure they align with your travel itinerary..."

Use:

"...to better understand where you'll be staying during your trip."

The goal is to sound like a helpful visa coach, not a government report.

4. Add encouragement throughout the report

Where appropriate, soften instructional language.

For example, instead of only saying:

Be prepared to explain...

consider wording like:

Don't worry if this comes up. Just be ready to explain...

or

If you're asked about this, simply explain...

Do this naturally without making every section sound repetitive.

The overall tone should help applicants feel supported and more confident.

5. Add a new section after the encouragement message

Insert a new section titled:

Strengths in Your Application

This section should highlight positive aspects found in the applicant's DS-160.

Examples include:

Clear purpose of travel
Stable employment
Well-defined travel plans
Identified source of trip funding
Complete accommodation plans
Consistent travel timeline

Only include strengths that genuinely exist in the applicant's application.

End the section with:

These strengths do not guarantee a visa decision, but they provide a solid foundation for your interview preparation.

This section should appear before Top Preparation Areas.

Guiding Principle

When writing customer-facing text:

Choose clarity over formality, reassurance over intimidation, and understanding over complexity. Every sentence should help applicants feel more prepared and more confident.
