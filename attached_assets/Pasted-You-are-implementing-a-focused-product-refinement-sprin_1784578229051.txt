==================================================
PRODUCT CONTEXT
==================================================

VisaPrep is an AI-powered visa preparation platform initially designed for Nigerians applying for U.S. visas.

Its core philosophy is:

Understanding before evaluation.
Familiarity before memorization.
Preparation, not prediction.

VisaPrep must help genuine applicants:

- Understand what their visa application communicates
- Recognize the parts of their application that deserve attention
- Prepare to explain their own circumstances clearly and truthfully
- Approach their interview with greater clarity and confidence

VisaPrep must never imply that it can predict visa approval, guarantee an outcome, manipulate an interview or help applicants deceive a consular officer.

The platform should feel:

- Trustworthy
- Intelligent
- Calm
- Personal
- Clear
- Supportive
- Premium but accessible

Avoid language that feels:

- Alarmist
- Accusatory
- Overly legalistic
- Robotic
- Judgmental
- Certain about what an officer will ask
- Certain about the outcome of an interview

Use plain English that is natural and understandable to Nigerian applicants.

==================================================
CENTRAL PRODUCT DIRECTION
==================================================

The product experience must clearly distinguish between understanding and preparation.

FREE = UNDERSTANDING

The free Application Insights experience helps the applicant understand what their application communicates and identify the most important parts that deserve attention.

PAID = PREPARATION

The paid experience helps the applicant prepare to explain those areas, understand possible interview implications, review personalized questions and build confidence.

Core commercial principle:

Never charge users merely to understand what the system has identified in their own application.

Charge for the deeper preparation required to respond clearly and confidently.

The free experience must provide genuine personalized value. However, it must not become a complete interview-preparation report.

The user should finish the free report feeling:

- Seen
- Informed
- Reassured
- Curious to continue
- Convinced that VisaPrep genuinely understood their application

The user should not finish feeling:

- Frightened
- Overwhelmed
- Judged
- Confused
- Fully prepared without needing the paid experience

4. SPLIT VALIDATION AND ANALYSIS INTO TWO STAGES
==================================================

Refine the analysis flow into two clearly separated internal stages.

STAGE 1: DOCUMENT VALIDATION

The system should first determine whether the uploaded file:

- Can be opened
- Contains usable text or extractable content
- Appears to be the relevant completed DS-160 material required by VisaPrep
- Contains enough information for meaningful analysis

During this stage, show only generic validation messages.

Examples:

- Uploading your document...
- Checking the file...
- Reading the document...
- Confirming the document type...
- Preparing your file for analysis...

Do not show DS-160-specific intelligence messages before validation succeeds.

If validation fails:

- Stop the process.
- Do not continue into DS-160 analysis.
- Display a calm, specific and helpful error.
- Explain what type of document is required.
- Allow the user to select another file.
- Do not use technical error language unless necessary.

Possible error structure:

“We couldn’t confirm that this file contains enough DS-160 information for analysis. Please upload a readable copy of your completed DS-160 application, or clear screenshots containing the application details.”

Adapt the wording to the actual file formats currently supported by the application.

STAGE 2: DS-160 ANALYSIS

Only after successful validation should the interface begin showing DS-160-specific progress messages.

Examples:

- Reviewing your application details...
- Understanding your travel plans...
- Examining important connections across your application...
- Identifying your application strengths...
- Preparing your personalized Application Insights...

Prevent progress messages from looping awkwardly during longer analyses. Spread them naturally across the process.

Preserve the current working analysis system wherever possible. Do not introduce unnecessary duplicate API calls.

12. SIMPLIFY EACH APPLICATION HIGHLIGHT
==================================================

3. A short application-specific explanation

Example format:

Employment Transition

Why this may come up

Your application shows that you recently moved from a long-term role at one company to a newer position. Because your current employment is relatively recent, you should be familiar with the timeline and how the change fits into your professional history.

However, be careful not to turn the free explanation into full preparation guidance.

The free version may explain why the detail is notable, but it should not provide:

- A prepared answer
- A response script
- A list of interview questions
- A coaching strategy
- A detailed explanation framework
- Instructions for overcoming perceived weaknesses

Also avoid certainty such as:

“The officer will ask about this.”

Prefer language such as:

- This may attract additional attention.
- This may naturally lead to questions.
- This is an important part of your application to understand.
- The combination of these details may require a clear explanation.
