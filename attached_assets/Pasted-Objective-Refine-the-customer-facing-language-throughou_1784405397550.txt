Objective

Refine the customer-facing language throughout the VisaPrep Application Insights experience. This is a writing and UX improvement only. Do not change the application logic, JSON schema, API structure, data extraction, or analysis flow.

Writing Philosophy

VisaPrep is designed to reduce uncertainty by helping applicants understand their own application.

Every piece of customer-facing text should make applicants feel:

encouraged
understood
supported
more confident

Never make them feel:

judged
interrogated
accused
intimidated

VisaPrep should sound like an experienced visa coach sitting beside the applicant, not like a government report or AI assistant.

Writing Guidelines

Rewrite customer-facing text using these rules:

1. Use simple everyday English.

Prefer words like:

shows
asks
understand
explain
prepare
ready

Avoid unnecessarily formal words like:

indicates
explore
assess
evaluate
personalized preparation
comprehensive
optimize
maximize
facilitate

If an ordinary Nigerian applicant would have to read a sentence twice, simplify it.

2. Speak directly to the applicant.

Whenever appropriate, use:

you
your application
your interview

instead of:

the applicant
the applicant's application

Example:

Instead of:

The applicant is applying...

Use:

You're applying...

3. Encourage throughout the experience.

The report should reassure applicants while remaining truthful.

Use supportive language such as:

Don't worry...
This is normal...
We'll help you...
This is simply something to prepare for...
Understanding this now can help you feel more confident during your interview.

Do not exaggerate or promise approval.

4. Avoid sounding accusatory.

Replace language that sounds investigative.

Examples:

Instead of:

A consular officer may explore this...

Use:

During your interview, you may be asked about this...

or

A consular officer may want to understand this better...

5. Remove unnecessary legal or technical wording.

Example:

Instead of:

refusal under section 214(b)

Use:

previous visa refusal

Applicants do not need legal terminology unless it genuinely helps them understand something.

6. Be consistent.

Prefer:

shows
understand
be ready to explain

Keep terminology consistent throughout the report.
