VisaPrep Product Intelligence Sprint
From Fact Extraction to Application Intelligence

Congratulations on completing the Product Refinement Sprint.

We have now evaluated the product against six benchmark applicants covering B1/B2 and F-1 visa categories. Overall, the product performs well and consistently delivers valuable Application Insights.

This sprint is not about changing VisaPrep's philosophy or architecture.

It is about making the intelligence noticeably deeper, the experience more personalized, and the value immediately obvious to users.

Core Product Philosophy

Continue preserving VisaPrep's guiding principle:

Your interview starts with your application. So do we.

VisaPrep exists to help genuine applicants understand what their application communicates and prepare to explain it clearly and confidently.

Do not predict visa outcomes.

Do not speculate about consular decisions.

Do not encourage applicants to game the interview.

The New Objective

The engine already identifies important facts well.

The next evolution is much more ambitious.

Teach the engine to understand the application as one connected story.

Rather than producing a collection of observations, produce meaningful insights.

An insight should help the applicant think:

"I never realized those parts of my application connected like that."

or

"Now I understand why this might naturally come up during my interview."

Every insight should deliver value that goes beyond simply reading the DS-160.

The applicant should feel they have learned something meaningful about their own application.

The Application Insights should provide value they are unlikely to receive from:

reading their DS-160
a typical visa consultant
generic interview question websites
a generic AI assistant

The free experience should naturally make users want to continue because they immediately recognize the depth of understanding VisaPrep provides.

1. Cross-Section Reasoning

Highest priority.

Move beyond identifying isolated facts.

Identify meaningful relationships between different parts of the application.

Examples include:

Employment ↔ Purpose of travel
Job responsibilities ↔ Conference attendance
Degree ↔ Master's programme
Internship ↔ Career goals
Scholarship ↔ Academic achievement
Previous refusal ↔ Changes reflected in the current application
Previous travel ↔ Current travel plans
Family information ↔ Interview preparation
Funding ↔ Nature of trip
Executive responsibilities ↔ Business meetings

Rather than saying:

You have stable employment.

Explain:

Your current role appears directly connected to the purpose of your trip. Be prepared to explain how attending this event supports your responsibilities at work.

The focus should shift from facts to meaning.

2. Increase Insight Value

Every Application Highlight should answer one question:

Why is this important for this applicant?

Avoid merely describing facts.

Instead explain:

why the applicant should understand it
how it connects to other parts of the application
why it may naturally become part of an interview conversation

The applicant should consistently feel:

"I hadn't looked at my application that way before."

3. Personalized Encouragement

Replace the generic encouragement.

Generate one short encouragement uniquely based on the applicant's own profile.

Use information such as:

academic achievements
career progression
professional growth
funding
scholarships
previous travel
executive experience
long-term goals

Each report should feel individually written.

4. Replace Low-Value Observations

Avoid observations that add little value unless stronger observations are unavailable.

Examples include:

Complete supporting documents
Clear purpose of travel
Clear purpose of study
Well-defined itinerary

Instead prioritize richer observations that demonstrate understanding.

5. Use More Available Context

Many applications contain valuable information that currently goes unused.

Examples include:

internships
promotions
management responsibilities
ownership interests
scholarships
academic distinctions
employment transitions
executive positions
board memberships
previous compliant travel

When they strengthen the overall narrative, incorporate them into the analysis.

6. Avoid Unsupported Inferences

Remain grounded.

Do not infer:

why a previous visa was refused
that a factor improves approval chances
that something weakens or strengthens an application
that a factor simplifies the application process

Discuss only what the application communicates.

7. Family Information

Where appropriate, acknowledge immediate family information factually.

Example:

Your application includes immediate family members in Nigeria. Be comfortable discussing your family circumstances if questions naturally arise during your interview.

Avoid speculation.

14. Golden Test Suite

The six benchmark applicants now become VisaPrep's permanent internal evaluation suite.

Future prompt refinements should be validated against all six applicants.

The objective is continual improvement across diverse applicant profiles rather than optimization for one scenario.

Success Criteria

This sprint is successful if users move from saying:

"The AI summarized my application."

to saying:

"The AI helped me understand my application in ways I hadn't considered, and now I see why preparing from my own application is so valuable."

Every insight should reinforce VisaPrep's unique promise:

Your interview starts with your application. So do we.
