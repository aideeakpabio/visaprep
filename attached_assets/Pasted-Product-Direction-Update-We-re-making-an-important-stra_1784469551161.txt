Product Direction Update

We're making an important strategic shift in VisaPrep.

The product should no longer emphasize AI analysis or the assessment itself.

The assessment is only the beginning.

VisaPrep's promise is to help users:

Understand your application. Prepare with clarity. Interview with confidence.

Everything in the product should reinforce that journey.

Our internal product journey is now:

1. Understand

Understand your application.

↓

2. Prepare

Prepare with clarity.

↓

3. Practice

Practice with AI.

↓

4. Interview

Interview with confidence.

Every screen, message, and CTA should reduce uncertainty and move users naturally from one stage to the next.

Landing Page

The current landing page asks users to upload a DS-160 immediately.

Instead, first establish empathy and communicate that VisaPrep understands what applicants actually need.

Keep the hero:

Your interview starts with your application. So do we.

Replace the supporting copy with messaging focused on outcomes rather than uploading PDFs.

Suggested direction:

Understand your application. Prepare with clarity. Interview with confidence.

Follow with a concise sentence communicating that VisaPrep helps applicants understand what their application communicates and prepare to explain it confidently during their interview.

The upload box should become the beginning of that journey rather than the main focus.

Instead of leading with:

Upload your completed DS-160...

Lead with the value.

Then invite the upload.

Application Insights (Free)

The free product remains Application Insights.

However, the emphasis should be on what users gain rather than the report itself.

Users should leave thinking:

"Finally, someone helped me understand my own application."

The report should clearly communicate:

what their application communicates
what they should be ready to explain
where they should focus their preparation
Paid Experience

Do not position the paid product as purchasing a "Full Assessment."

Instead, position it as beginning a complete preparation journey.

Replace the current payment messaging with something like:

Start Your Personalized Interview Preparation

Immediately after payment, you'll begin your personalized preparation with:

✅ Comprehensive Application Assessment

Understand the strengths, inconsistencies, and interview implications of your application.

✅ Personalized Interview Preparation

Know exactly what parts of YOUR application require the most attention.

✅ Personalized Interview Questions

Questions generated specifically from your own DS-160.

✅ Areas You Should Be Ready to Explain

Know what a consular officer is most likely to explore further.

✅ Personalized Preparation Roadmap

A clear plan showing what to review before your interview.

✅ AI Interview Practice (Coming Soon)

Practice realistic visa interview conversations based on your own application and receive personalized feedback after every session.

Do NOT use the word "Mock."

Use "Practice."

Primary CTA

Replace the payment button text.

Instead of emphasizing access to an assessment, emphasize beginning preparation.

Preferred direction:

Start My Personalized Interview Preparation

Alternative acceptable wording:

Start My Complete Interview Preparation

The CTA should feel like the beginning of a journey rather than unlocking a report.

Product Philosophy

Throughout the product, remember:

People are not paying VisaPrep for information.

They are paying for confidence.

Information creates understanding.

Understanding creates clarity.

Clarity enables preparation.

Preparation builds confidence.

VisaPrep never promises visa approval.

VisaPrep promises better preparation.

Every screen should reduce uncertainty.

Users should leave each interaction feeling more prepared than before.

The goal is for applicants to think:

"These people understand exactly what I've been looking for."
